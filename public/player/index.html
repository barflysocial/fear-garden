0:00–0:45

The Garden Goes Quiet

Mid City Beer Garden was built for noise: chairs scraping across brick floors, glasses touching down on wood tables, laughter moving under the hanging lights, and the soft buzz of a Baton Rouge night settling into the garden patio.

Tonight was supposed to be a private preview for Fear Garden, a live detective mystery experience where guests would scan in, follow clues, and solve a fictional crime before last call.

But at 9:42 PM, the fake mystery stopped mattering. A real body was found near the rear courtyard service gate, close enough to the event setup that several guests thought it was part of the show.

0:45–1:30

The Victim: Everett Vale

The victim was Everett Vale, a nightlife consultant, promoter, and dealmaker who had spent six months trying to turn Baton Rouge’s bar scene into his own personal empire.

Everett knew owners, bartenders, musicians, vendors, influencers, investors, and staff. More importantly, he knew their secrets. If someone owed money, Everett knew. If someone lied on an invoice, he knew. If someone planned to cut a partner out of a deal, he usually knew that too.

To the public, Everett was charming. He smiled for photos and spoke about community. Behind closed doors, he collected leverage and used it like currency.

1:30–2:15

The Night Turns Real

Everett was last seen entering the garden patio at 9:18 PM. Minutes later, the camera watching the back walkway cut out for exactly seven minutes. When the feed returned, Everett was already on the ground.

His phone was face down beside him. One glass lay broken nearby. A folded paper napkin was tucked halfway beneath his hand. On it, someone had written: “THE GARDEN KEEPS WHAT IT HEARS.”

No weapon was found. No one claims to have seen the murder. And the first officers on scene noticed the strangest part: the evidence did not point to one clear suspect. It pointed everywhere.

2:15–3:30

The Suspects

Mara Lane, the event coordinator, built tonight’s Fear Garden launch from the ground up. She expected a producer credit and a share of future ticket sales. Everett’s printed contract removed her name completely. Witnesses heard her say, “You do not get to bury me in my own event.”

Dante Reed, the garden bartender, had history with Everett. Years ago, a promoted pop-up collapsed, vendors went unpaid, and Dante’s reputation took the hit. Tonight, a drink was comped with Dante’s employee code at 9:21 PM. Dante says he did not comp it.

Selene Price, a local influencer, needed Everett’s promotion but feared his files. Everett had screenshots that could expose her for charging venues for “organic” promotion while taking money from competitors to bury their events. After the body was found, witnesses saw Selene deleting messages.

3:30–4:15

More Pressure Points

Grant Bell, the investor, had money tied to Everett’s next deal. Everett promised him ownership points, sponsor access, and a clean launch, but Grant recently learned Everett was shopping the same concept to another investor. Grant says he was in the front bar, but a witness remembers his gold watch near the garden hallway.

Lila Moreau, an antique vendor and prop supplier, provided old keys, framed photos, weathered books, antique bottles, and a locked wooden box for the game. Everett’s notes list her name beside the phrase: “Circa connection — pressure point.” Lila says some doors should stay closed.

Noah Voss, Everett’s assistant, handled calendars, payments, guest lists, files, and private messages. He says he was printing extra clue sheets during the murder window, but the printer log says the last print job happened before 9:10 PM.

4:15–5:00

Your Mission

Everett’s phone may be the key. At 9:36 PM, four minutes before the body was discovered, a message was sent from his phone: “IF I GO DOWN, THEY ALL GO DOWN.” Medical timing suggests Everett may already have been dead when that message was sent.

That means either the timeline is wrong, or someone used Everett’s phone after killing him. Your first questions are simple: who had access during the camera blackout, who knew enough about the fake mystery to hide a real murder inside it, and who needed Everett silenced before he exposed what he knew?

You are not here to chase rumors. You are here to separate performance from truth. When the countdown ends, your 30-minute investigation begins. Open each investigation app as evidence unlocks. Compare timelines, statements, messages, maps, money, and motive. The garden remembers everything.
